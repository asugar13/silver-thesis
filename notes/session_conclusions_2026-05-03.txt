ON THE DATA AND FREQUENCY
--------------------------
- Switched from daily 1-step-ahead to weekly 5-step-ahead forecasting.
  Created 03b_arima_daily.ipynb to keep daily analysis alongside the weekly one.
  The exog problem (needing future values of gold/USD/copper/S&P500 to forecast
  multiple steps ahead) is what made weekly aggregation the right choice for ARIMAX.

- Log returns are additive so resample('W-FRI').sum() gives exact weekly returns
  — no approximation involved.
  Weekly log return = sum of 5 daily log returns (exact identity, not an estimate).

- Sentiment aggregated with .mean() (not .sum()) since scores are bounded [-1, 1]
  and summing would produce an unbounded number with no clear interpretation.


ON WEEKLY SILVER RETURNS
-------------------------
- ACF/PACF completely flat at all lags.
- Ljung-Box test: p = 0.358 — no significant autocorrelation up to lag 20.
- AIC grid search selects ARIMA(0,0,0) — constant mean with white noise.
- Weekly silver log-returns are white noise.

- This is consistent with weak-form EMH (Efficient Market Hypothesis):
  past prices are already fully reflected in the current price.

- This actually strengthens the thesis narrative: if ARIMA is useless,
  all predictive power must come from external variables (market co-movements
  and sentiment) — which is exactly what the thesis tests.

- Ad-hoc order comparison (ARIMA(0,0,0), (0,0,2), (2,0,0) vs AIC-best)
  belongs in the daily notebook (03b), motivated by the daily ACF/PACF lag-2
  spike. At weekly frequency no order comparison is needed — white noise wins.


ON SENTIMENT VS RETURNS (SCATTER ANALYSIS)
--------------------------------------------
- Reddit sentiment: r ≈ 0 at all frequencies and lags. All p > 0.30.
  Completely insignificant. No raw linear relationship with silver returns.

- News sentiment (FinBERT):
    - Daily (both lags): r ≈ 0.00, p > 0.50. Nothing.
    - Weekly lagged: r = -0.066, p = 0.294. Not significant.
    - Weekly contemporaneous: r = 0.178, p = 0.005. Significant — BUT:
        (a) r² = 0.178² ≈ 3.2% of variance explained — practically negligible.
        (b) Not usable for forecasting: you cannot observe the full week's
            news aggregate before the week ends.
        (c) Likely reflects news reacting to prices, not causing them.

- Rolling 52-week correlation (lagged Reddit vs weekly silver returns):
    - Stays near zero throughout most of the sample.
    - Goes NEGATIVE around and after the January 2021 squeeze.
    - Interpretation: Reddit was overwhelmingly bullish during the squeeze
      but prices fell sharply afterward — delayed mean-reversion, not a
      leading indicator.


ON STATISTICAL VS PRACTICAL SIGNIFICANCE
-----------------------------------------
- r  = Pearson correlation coefficient (direction and strength, -1 to +1)
- r² = coefficient of determination = percentage of variance explained

  r = 0.178  →  r² = 0.032  →  3.2% of variance explained
  r = 0.5    →  r² = 0.25   →  25% of variance explained
  r = 0.9    →  r² = 0.81   →  81% of variance explained

- With n=255 weekly observations, even tiny effects can reach p<0.05.
  Statistical significance ≠ practical significance.
  Always report r² alongside p-value for financial data.


ON WHY SCATTER AND ARIMAX CAN DISAGREE
----------------------------------------
- Scatter tests the raw correlation between sentiment and silver returns
  in isolation — ignoring all other variables.

- ARIMAX tests whether sentiment explains residual variation LEFT OVER
  after gold, USD, copper and S&P500 are already accounted for.

- These are two different questions. It is not a contradiction if scatter
  shows r≈0 while ARIMAX shows a small marginal improvement.

- Market co-movements already explain ~70% of silver's weekly direction.
  Sentiment might correlate with the unexplained 30% even if it has no
  raw correlation with the total.

ON THE LOOK-AHEAD BIAS ILLUSTRATION (03b_arima_daily.ipynb)
-------------------------------------------------------------
- Look-ahead version uses same-day exog (gold/USD/copper/S&P500 closing
  prices on day t to predict silver return on day t).
- This is a bias: you cannot know gold's closing price on day t before
  silver also closes on day t.
- Kept in 03b as an illustration — the DA/WDA gap between look-ahead and
  lagged versions quantifies exactly how much the bias inflates metrics.
- Look-ahead version is excluded from all model comparisons in 07_evaluation.


ON THE LSTM NOTEBOOK (05_lstm.ipynb — rewritten)
--------------------------------------------------
Major bug found and fixed:
- Original predictions were not inverse-transformed from z-score space
  back to log-return space. RMSE and MAE were in standardized units,
  completely incomparable to ARIMA metrics. DA was still correct (scaling
  preserves sign) but magnitude metrics were wrong.

New structure — 4 variants mirroring ARIMA exactly:
  LSTM-Y       silver_return only              (equivalent to ARIMA)
  LSTM-EXOG    silver_return + market vars      (equivalent to ARIMAX)
  LSTM-REDDIT  LSTM-EXOG + reddit_sentiment     (equivalent to ARIMAX+Reddit)
  LSTM-NEWS    LSTM-EXOG + news_sentiment       (equivalent to ARIMAX+News)

Key advantage of LSTM over ARIMAX for sentiment:
- ARIMAX uses only t-1 sentiment (one lag).
- LSTM receives the full 20-day history of sentiment scores in its input
  sequence. It can learn multi-lag, non-linear patterns that ARIMAX misses.

Also fixed: MAPE removed (unreliable for log returns near zero),
predictions saved with date index for alignment in 07_evaluation.


OVERALL THESIS DIRECTION
-------------------------
- The retail hypothesis has NO linear support at any frequency tested
  (daily or weekly, ARIMAX or scatter).
- The LSTM is the remaining test for non-linear, multi-lag sentiment effects.
- If LSTM also finds nothing: clean conclusion — retail sentiment is not a
  predictive signal at daily or weekly frequency in linear or non-linear models.
- The thesis framing shifts from "sentiment drives silver" to "we carefully
  tested whether sentiment drives silver and found no evidence at these
  frequencies" — which is a legitimate and publishable finding.

EMH framing:
- Weak-form EMH holds: no autocorrelation, ARIMA useless.
- Semi-strong EMH: public sentiment data (news, Reddit) does not appear
  to be mispriced at daily or weekly frequency — ARIMAX and DM tests confirm.
- LSTM tests the non-linear semi-strong hypothesis.
